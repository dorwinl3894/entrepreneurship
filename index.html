Entrepreneurship

Learn to build a business from scratch!

What is Entrepreneurship?

Entrepreneurship is the honest living process of creating a small business into a successful one, alongside taking many risks in order to make profit. Most famous and highly profitable companies began with an entrepreneur who came up with a brilliant idea.

Steve Jobs
The Co-founder of Apple Inc started his business in his garage. It is now a multinational technology company making $50 billion every year.

Daymond John
Founded his company "For Us, By Us" at his home in Queens where he practiced and learned to sew hats in a way that no one has ever done before. His clothing line company is now worth more than a billion dollars and he holds a net worth of $250 million dollars.

Mark Zuckerberg
Co-founded Facebook in his college dorm with a simple but genius idea of a way to connect college students with each other much easier. Now, he has reshaped his company so that anybody can have a space in Facebook. These ideas, work ethic, and risks are what made him one of the richest people in the world.

>The Start

A successful entrepreneur always has a plan to start their business.

Think about something that people will want or need to have.


Most successful entrepreneurs began their business because they realized something was missing in society.

  • “What does the world need that doesn’t exist yet?”
  • "“Can ____ be made more efficiently?"

Do your research online and get educated



If something you thought of is not unique or useful, the chances of making a successful business out of it is little to none.



If you thought of something completely new and ingenious, research how you can make it into a reality!



Some of the questions you can ask yourself while researching is…

  • “Do I need knowledge in a specific field of interest?”
  • “Am I going to work alone or team up with someone who is capable of making this business into a reality?”

>The Build

Start building a prototype of your idea.


Some ideas may require money to build, and if that's the case, consider partnering up with someone who believes in your idea and is able to fund your project.


This phase is going to require lots of hustling, so don’t give up because this might just be your start of a successful future!

Now that you have a prototype, it is time to let the world know about your business.


Marketing can be used in many different ways.

  • Digital Marketing - Youtube, Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, etc

  • Event Marketing - go to a niche convention that fits your business and sign up to present your new business.

  • Interruption marketing - buy advertisement space in radio, websites, and mail to spread your business.

>Growth

Depending on the type of business you have created, you might need to create inventory.

Inventory is how much of your product you currently have on you ready to be shipped out.

You will probably not need to create an inventory if your business does not require anything physical.

  • Mobile App, Videos, Software

Now that you have a business, the only thing left is to grow it out.

Make sure you are still putting work into your business so that your profits go up and your business flourishes.


Make rational decisions so that you are always making more money than you are using.


Even if you do not see clear growth or profit in your business, it doesn’t mean it wont work.

  • Tesla Motors was incorporated in 2003 and it was only until 2013 that it experienced a year of profit!
